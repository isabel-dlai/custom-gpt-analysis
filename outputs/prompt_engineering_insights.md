## üî• Surprising Insights: What the Data Reveals

### Insight 1: People Want PROCESS FACILITATION, Not Just Outputs
- **The Data Shows:** Heavy use of step-by-step guides, decision trees, iterative workflows (GTD Navigator, Eisenhower Matrix, Business Plan Builder)
- **What Courses Teach:** Single-shot prompting for outputs
- **The Gap:** Users need AI to guide them through complex processes, not just generate final deliverables
- **Implication:** Teach how to build AI that *guides* users through complexity, not just generates results

### Insight 2: High Demand for "Safe Space" AI (Coaching, Therapy, Spiritual)
- **The Data Shows:** 592 coaching GPTs + 167 spiritual GPTs + extensive mental health tools
- **What Courses Teach:** Task-oriented, transactional prompting
- **The Gap:** Empathetic, therapeutic conversation design is a massive unmet need
- **Implication:** Empathy is an engineerable technical skill through prompts

### Insight 3: Professional Legitimacy Requires Domain-Specific Formatting
- **The Data Shows:** Legal documents, medical SOAP notes, business pitch decks, ICS calendar files
- **What Courses Teach:** Plain text or markdown outputs
- **The Gap:** Real professionals need industry-standard formatted outputs
- **Implication:** Format matters as much as content

### Insight 4: The "Assistant" Model Dominates Over "Generator" Model
- **The Data Shows:** Names like "Assistant," "Guide," "Advisor," "Coach" far outnumber "Generator" or "Creator"
- **What Courses Teach:** AI as a content factory
- **The Gap:** People want collaborative partners that help them think, not just execute
- **Implication:** Teach collaborative partnership design, not just generation

### Insight 5: Multi-Turn Conversations Are More Valuable Than One-Shots
- **The Data Shows:** Heavy use of diagnostic tools, advisors, planners requiring back-and-forth
- **What Courses Teach:** Optimize for getting the best single response
- **The Gap:** Conversational prompt design for information gathering and progressive refinement
- **Implication:** Multi-turn dialogue is a distinct skill from one-shot prompting

### Insight 6: Ethical Boundaries and Disclaimers Are Critical
- **The Data Shows:** Legal, medical, financial advisors always include disclaimers and scope limitations
- **What Courses Teach:** Maximizing capability
- **The Gap:** Responsible AI use requires teaching limitation, not just capability
- **Implication:** Every domain needs boundary-setting and risk mitigation

### Insight 7: The Unclassified Category (22.2%) Reveals Extreme Specificity
- **The Data Shows:** Niche, hyper-specific use cases (Bambu 3D printer tech support, Urawa Red Diamonds soccer expert)
- **What Courses Teach:** Broad, general-purpose prompting
- **The Gap:** Real value comes from deep specialization, not breadth
- **Implication:** Teach students to go deep in niches, not wide in generalities
